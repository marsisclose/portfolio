Propeller Shaft Design for Nuclear-Powered Submarines

CourseStrength of Materials II
ToolsMATLAB
DateApril 2025
MATLAB Fatigue Analysis Stress Analysis Torsional Analysis DFM

Overview

Designed a propeller shaft for a nuclear-powered submarine to satisfy simultaneous torque, deflection, and fatigue constraints under operational loading conditions. The goal was to optimize material selection and shaft geometry while maintaining structural and dynamic integrity across the full operating envelope.

Problem Statement

Submarine propeller shafts operate under high sustained torque, bending loads from propeller weight and hydrodynamic forces, and fatigue cycling over decades of service. The design had to meet strict limits on:

  • Maximum shear stress under full-power torque loading
  • Static deflection at the propeller end under combined loading
  • Fatigue life using a Goodman-based safety factor approach
  • Critical speed — avoiding resonance within the operating RPM range

Approach

Analysis was conducted entirely in MATLAB, building a parametric model that allowed rapid iteration across shaft diameter, material, and bearing placement. Key analyses included:

  • Torsional stress and angle of twist calculations under peak torque
  • Beam deflection modeling with shaft treated as a simply-supported beam under distributed and point loads
  • Critical speed calculation using Rayleigh's method to identify resonance risk
  • Bearing reaction loads and contact stress verification
  • Fatigue analysis using the modified Goodman diagram with appropriate surface finish, stress concentration, and reliability correction factors
