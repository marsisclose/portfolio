Overview

As a member of RIT Launch Initiative's Liquids Propulsion Team, I contribute to the design and analysis of a 320 lbf thrust liquid bipropellant rocket engine using isopropyl alcohol (IPA) as fuel and nitrous oxide (N₂O) as oxidizer. The engine is being developed for eventual hot-fire testing and integration into a full launch vehicle.

My Role

My work focuses on the thrust chamber and nozzle subsystem. Responsibilities include:

  • Nozzle contour design — defining throat area, expansion ratio, and exit geometry to maximize specific impulse at target altitude
  • Heat transfer analysis — modeling heat flux through the combustion chamber wall and nozzle throat to verify material limits and inform cooling requirements
  • Combustion performance analysis — CEA-based predictions for chamber temperature, specific impulse, and characteristic velocity
  • CAD modeling of nozzle and thrust chamber geometry in collaboration with the manufacturing team
  • Structural analysis of chamber wall under internal pressure and thermal loading

Previously served as a Structures Engineer on Level 2 solid rockets — managing fin geometry, stability calculations, and aerodynamic performance for a vehicle targeting 6,000 ft apogee.
