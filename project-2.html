Lunar Lander Descent Simulation

CourseIntro to Coding for Engineers
ToolsMATLAB

MATLAB Kinematics Simulation Optimization Numerical Methods

Overview

Developed a MATLAB simulation of a lunar lander during powered descent, modeling the vehicle's kinematics, thrust control, and fuel consumption from initial altitude through touchdown. The objective was to achieve a controlled soft landing while minimizing total fuel expenditure.

Problem Statement

Lunar descent is a constrained optimization problem: too little thrust and the lander impacts at unsafe velocity; too much and fuel runs out before touchdown. The simulation had to model:

  • Variable thrust as a control input, affecting both deceleration and fuel burn rate
  • Decreasing vehicle mass as propellant is consumed (Tsiolkovsky rocket equation basis)
  • Lunar gravitational acceleration (1.62 m/s²) as the only acting body force
  • Terminal velocity constraint — soft landing defined as impact velocity below 2 m/s

Approach

The simulation used numerical integration (Euler method) to propagate the vehicle state — altitude, velocity, mass — through each time step. Key elements:

  • Equations of motion derived from Newton's second law with variable mass
  • Thrust profile parameterized and swept across design space to minimize fuel use
  • Constraint checking at each time step for velocity and altitude bounds
  • Visualization of descent trajectory, velocity, and fuel remaining over time
